The Unknown Soldier Review


Robert Cady

October 23, 2025

After watching this movie a few times... I can safely say this may be one of the best war movies I've seen. It takes a great look into the Continuation War from a Finnish perspective, and doesn't shy away from the brutal reality of fighting in these battles. The film follows a freshly graduated machine gun company as they get ready to embark on their journey to reclaim Finnish territory from the Soviet Union. The thing I like most about this movie, is throughout the entire film you see the genuine effects of prolonged combat on young men, and the disconnect from high-ranking officers and junior officers/enlisted soldiers. Despite this being a Finnish movie, the entire time I knew exactly what was going on, and the emotions they were feeling.

Trivia

  • This movie is based of Väinö Linna's book The Unkown Soldier, and his own experiences as a soldier in the Finnish army during the Continuation War (1941-1944)
  • Nearly every actor in this movie has actually served in the Finnish Army
  • The film was released the same year that Finland celebrated its 100th anniversary of independence
